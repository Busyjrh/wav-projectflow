07 · Risks & Questions

Resolve the problems that are easiest to miss before they become production problems.

This page separates known risks from questions that require an answer from Nathaniel, Jessica, or REDCAT.

Contextual risk register

Arrival after rehearsalYou arrive at 5:00 PM after the 1:45–4:00 PM rehearsal. Ask for a short blocking, cue, and stage-change briefing immediately.
House opens 30 minutes after your callTripod placement, cable control, screen brightness, card checks, and audible setup must be completed quickly and discreetly.
Two different visual problemsThe sextet spreads six musicians across the stage. Cut Piece concentrates attention on a 4-by-4-foot platform and audience interaction. One fixed framing strategy will not serve both equally well.
Performance duration ambiguityThe Cut Piece plot allows a broad duration while the schedule blocks a full hour. Build cards and batteries around the longer possibility.
Continuous-record storage growthHigher-bitrate or ProRes capture can multiply storage quickly. Confirm whether every camera truly needs to roll continuously.
Audience interaction and sensitivityConfirm framing expectations, participant visibility, and any boundaries for clothing changes or close audience interaction.
Media ownership at wrapKnow whether Nathaniel takes the cards, copies the footage, supplies media, or expects you to retain and upload it.
No live connected dataThis standalone file cannot automatically verify traffic, parking changes, weather, calendar conflicts, or family contacts.

Show the full value of the work, then discount intentionally.

This project should not look like only three hours of camera operation. Build the real scope first—production, prep, media handling, equipment reset, and travel—then apply a visible courtesy or relationship discount to reach the agreed $750.

Pricing strategy for this shoot

Recommended method Build the invoice at its real value of $902.50, then add a Relationship / Budget Adjustment of –$152.50 so the final amount due is exactly $750.00.

This teaches you what the work is actually worth, preserves your normal rates, and makes the discount visible instead of quietly training the client to think the complete job only costs $750.
